# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive information.
This usually happens through fake calls, messages, or links that appear to be from banks or trusted sources.

In a typical scenario, a student receives a call claiming that they have won a reward or need to verify their bank account.
The fraudster sends a payment request or a malicious link and asks the victim to approve it.
Without realizing, the victim enters their UPI PIN or clicks the link, resulting in money being transferred to the attacker’s account.

Students and inexperienced users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. 
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes loss of personal data.

This type of cybercrime is increasing rapidly, especially with the growing use of digital payments.
Awareness and careful behavior are essential to prevent such incidents.
